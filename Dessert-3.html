It has it's origins in medieval times when dried meats and fruits were stored in a pastry bag. When liquid was added to the dried mix, it expanded and was cooked like a pie to serve many people. Over the years, the dried fruit stuck around in addition to sugars, spices, and suet or animal fat. What started as something savory, is now a dessert centerpiece enjoyed by many in the UK.

Ingredients

  • Butter to grease
  • 450 g mixed dried fruit - 150 g golden raisins,150 g apricots, 150 g cherries, chopped
  • 100 g medjool dates, chopped
  • 50 g candied ginger, chopped
  • Zest from 1/2 orange
  • Juice of 1/2 orange
  • 125 g suet or beef fat
  • 125 g plain flour
  • 125 g dermera sugar
  • 150 g fresh breadcrumbs
  • 40 grams chopped walnuts
  • 2 g nutmeg, 2 g cinnamon, 2 g allspice
  • 2 g Coarse Kosher Salt
  • 1 egg
  • 150 ml stout or stale beer

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Grease a bowl with butter to hold the pudding (Small ceramic / pyrex heat safe bowl). Set aside.
  2. Remove pits from the dates. Roughly chop the dried fruit, dates, and candied ginger and place in a large mixing bowl.
  3. Finely grate in the orange zest, then squeeze in the juice.
  4. Add in the suet, flour, sugar, breadcrumbs, chopped walnuts, and spices. Loosely mix.
  5. Crack in the egg and pour in the beer. Mix with your hands or a spatula until the mixture holds together.
  6. Transfer the pudding mixture to the greased bowl from earlier and cover with a double layer of tin foil. Tie a piece of string around the side of the bowl to hold the tin foil tight.
  7. Place the pudding bowl in a large saucepan, and pour in enough water until it comes halfway up the bowl.
  8. Bring to the boil, place a tight-fitting lid on the pan, and simmer for 3 hours. Check the water regularly, making sure that it never boils dry. The bowl could crack and burn if it does.
  9. After 3 hours, remove the foil, and turn the pudding upside down on a plate. (Optional) Traditionally, the pudding can be aged for several weeks before Christmas in a cool, dry place.
  10. To serve, cover the pudding with brandy and light aflame. Serve with fresh cream.
