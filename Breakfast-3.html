Whiskey gravy paired with some roasted shallot mashed potatoes could be the perfect accouterment for your turkey this year.

Roasted Shallots

  • 3 Red Shallots
  • 42 g Butter (3 pieces)
  • Coarse Salt to taste
  • Black Pepper to taste

Mashed Potatoes

  • 1400 g Gold Potatoes
  • 42 g Butter
  • 120 g Milk
  • Dollop Sour Cream
  • Coarse Salt to taste
  • Black Pepper to taste

Whiskey Gravy

  • Turkey Neck + Trimmings
  • 28 g Butter
  • 10 g Carrot
  • 10 g Onion
  • 10 g Celery
  • 1 Sprig Rosemary
  • 25 g Flour
  • 25 g Whiskey
  • 500 g Chicken Stock
  • Coarse Salt to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

Roasted Shallots

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees fahrenheit (204 C).
  2. Place the unpeeled shallots with the butter, salt and pepper in a small cast iron or oven safe pan.
  3. Roast the shallots until they are soft and a knife easily slides through the interior (40-50 minutes).
  4. While shallots are roasting move on to the mashed potatoes and gravy...and enjoy the smells wafting throughout the kitchen.
  5. Once roasted, remove the outer skin, and roughly dice.

Mashed Potatoes

  1. Fill a large pot with water and add a healthy handful of salt, approximately 10 grams of salt per 1000 grams of water, a 1% solution. Add in the cubed potatoes
  2. Bring the water to a boil and cook until the potatoes are fork tender, about 20 minutes.
  3. Drain the potatoes and rice (or mash) potatoes into a bowl.
  4. Add the roasted and diced shallots, butter, milk, dollop of sour cream, salt + pepper to taste.
  5. Mix everything together and adjust the taste / texture as you see fit.

Whiskey Gravy

  1. Heat a pan with the butter over medium heat. Add the turkey neck and trimmings in the pan and brown them for 4-5 minutes, flipping the pieces once or twice.
  2. Add in the diced carrot, onion, celery, rosemary, a pinch or two of salt and saute for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the flour to create a roux. Cook for 2-3 minutes until it smells like a pie crust.
  4. Deglaze the pan by stirring in the whiskey and immediately add the chicken stock.
  5. Let the gravy simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring frequently, until it thickens. Taste the gravy. Season with salt and pepper. Strain out the turkey parts and vegetables with a mesh strainer and transfer to a gravy boat.
  6. Serve over the shallot mashed potatoes.
