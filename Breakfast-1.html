Soft scrambled eggs are my personal favorite and typically the ones I make at home. They are characterized by small or medium-sized cards cooked over low heat that end up tender, moist, and creamy without using a ton of butter.

Ingredients

  • 4 Eggs
  • 20 g Butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper (topping)
  • Chives (topping)

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. To start, add 4 eggs to a bowl along with a pinch of salt whisk vigorously together.
  2. Set a pan over low heat and drop the butter in the cold pan. Swirl the butter once to coat the pan and ensure it is not bubbling or else too hot. Pour in the eggs and stir slowly while the curds start to set, again stir more for smaller curds, less for slightly bigger ones.
  3. Then just before they reach my preferred level I take the pan off the heat and let the residual heat bring them to where I want them. You can always put it back on.
  4. Then layer the eggs onto a plate, and I hit them with a couple cranks of black pepper and some fresh chives.
